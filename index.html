Frequently Asked Questions About Nigeria's Grid

Q1: Is There Light in Nigeria Today?

Q2: Has Nigeria's National Grid Collapsed?

Q3: Why Do GenCos Report Low Gas and Power Supply?
A: Generation companies (GenCos) often face gas supply shortages, pipeline vandalism, or maintenance issues. This directly reduces power available to the national grid, affecting DisCo allocations and causing load shedding. Check GencoWatchNG for generation data.
Q4: How Does Nigeria's National Grid Work?
A: Nigeria's grid operates on three levels: GenCos generate power, TCN transmits it through national lines, and DisCos distribute to consumers. When any part fails, it affects the entire system. See the visual breakdown below.
Q5: What Does "Total MW" Mean on This Dashboard?
A: The total MW (megawatts) shown is the sum of power allocated to all 11 DisCos at the recorded time. This represents the actual power distributed, not generation capacity. Nigeria's generation capacity is higher, but transmission and distribution limitations reduce this number.
Q6: Where Can I Find National Grid News Today?
A: This dashboard provides real-time grid status based on official NIGRID data. For breaking news, follow TCN official channels. Our data is updated twice daily at 8-9am and 6-7pm.
